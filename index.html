First Trimester Nutrition Guide

A curated Nigerian food guide for a healthy and nourished first trimester.

Weeks 1 – 12

Essential Nutrients

Key vitamins and minerals your body needs, paired with local Nigerian foods.

🥬

Folic Acid

Prevents neural tube defects
Ugu (pumpkin leaves), efo tete, beans, plantain, green leafy vegetables
🩸

Iron

Supports blood volume
Dark leafy greens, liver, locust beans (iru/dawadawa), red meat, beans
🦴

Calcium

Bone & teeth formation
Crayfish, okra, ogbono seeds, sardines, nono (fermented milk)
☀️

Vitamin D

Bone development & immunity
Sunlight, titus fish (mackerel), eggs, sardines
🐟

DHA / Omega-3

Brain & eye development
Titus fish, sardines, crayfish, stockfish
🍌

Vitamin B6

Eases morning sickness
Yam, sweet potatoes, chicken, groundnuts, bananas
🧂

Iodine

Thyroid & brain support
Crayfish, prawns, iodized salt, eggs, seafood
💪

Zinc

Cell growth & immunity
Beef, chicken, egusi (pumpkin seeds), beans, groundnuts

Weekly Meal Plan

Delicious, nutrient-packed Nigerian meals for every day of the week.

🌙

Monday

Breakfast
Akamu (pap) with moi-moi
Lunch
Jollof rice with titus fish & plantain
Dinner
Efo riro with pounded yam
Snack
Oranges & groundnuts
🌿

Tuesday

Breakfast
Boiled yam with egg sauce
Lunch
Beans porridge with ripe plantain
Dinner
Egusi soup with semolina & stockfish
Snack
Watermelon & tiger nuts
🌸

Wednesday

Breakfast
Moi-moi with pap
Lunch
Fried rice with chicken & side salad
Dinner
Edikang ikong soup with fufu & assorted meat
Snack
Pawpaw & roasted groundnuts
🍃

Thursday

Breakfast
Boiled sweet potato with fish stew
Lunch
Yam porridge (asaro) with spinach & titus fish
Dinner
Oha soup with pounded yam & beef
Snack
Banana & boiled eggs
✨

Friday

Breakfast
Akamu with akara (bean cakes)
Lunch
Rice & beans with stew & plantain
Dinner
Ogbono soup with semolina & crayfish
Snack
Garden egg with groundnut paste
🌻

Saturday

Breakfast
Bread with scrambled eggs & sardines
Lunch
Coconut jollof rice with grilled chicken
Dinner
Fisherman pepper soup with yam
Snack
Coconut water & chin-chin (small portion)
🌞

Sunday

Breakfast
Yam & pepper sauce with boiled egg
Lunch
Ofada rice with ayamase (designer stew)
Dinner
Vegetable soup (ugu & waterleaf) with amala & fish
Snack
Fruit salad (watermelon, orange, pawpaw, banana)

💡 Quick Tips for the First Trimester

✓

Drink ginger tea to help manage morning sickness and nausea.

✓

Eat small, frequent meals every 2–3 hours instead of three large ones.

✓

Stay hydrated with water, coconut water, or zobo (in moderation).

✓

Akamu/pap is your best friend on tough nausea days — gentle and easy to digest.

✓

Include at least one leafy green vegetable in your meals every day.

✓

Take your prenatal vitamins alongside meals for better absorption.
